How to spot this specific attack

1. The Sender Address

The email claimed to be "Admin User," but the actual address was:
info.laverdad.edu@gmail.com

Official LVCC emails will never come from a @gmail.com account. Always look for @laverdad.edu.ph.

2. The Fake Website URL

Phishers create URLs that look almost identical. Compare these two carefully:

Real: lms.laverdad.edu.ph
Fake: lms-lvcc-edu-ph.vercel.app

Our simulation used "vercel.app" — a common hosting service hackers use to launch fake pages quickly.

How Fake Login Pages Work

Hackers use tools to clone the exact look and feel of real websites like our LMS. When you enter your credentials on a fake page:

  • Your username and password are saved directly into the hacker's database.
  • The hacker can then use your account to steal personal data or impersonate you to send more phishing emails.
  • In this drill, no actual passwords were stored; the system simply registered that the "Login" button was clicked.

General Security Tips

Hover before you click

On a computer, hover your mouse over any button or link. Preview the destination in the corner of your browser. If it doesn't match the sender, don't trust it.

Beware of "Urgent" Requests

Hackers use fear (e.g., "Account suspended!"). Official school notifications will never threaten you to force a quick password entry.

Never log in from an email link

Instead of clicking links in emails, manually type lms.laverdad.edu.ph in your browser to ensure you are on the legitimate site.

Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Enable 2-step verification where possible. This acts as a second lock that hackers cannot open even if they have your password.
