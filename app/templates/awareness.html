Don't worry, you are not in trouble! This was an authorized cybersecurity drill conducted by the IT Department. However, if this had been a real attack, your credentials or school data could have been compromised.

How to spot a phishing email next time:

  • 🚩
    Check the sender's actual email address. Scammers can fake the display name, but the underlying email address usually looks strange.
  • 🚩
    Hover before you click. Hover your mouse over any links to see the real destination URL before clicking.
  • 🚩
    Beware of urgency. Phishing emails try to panic you (e.g., "Your password expires in 2 hours!").

Remember: IT will never ask you to verify your password via a random email link.
